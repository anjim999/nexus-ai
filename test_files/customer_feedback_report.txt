CUSTOMER SATISFACTION & CHURN RISK ANALYSIS - Q1 2026

## 1. SEGMENTATION & ENGAGEMENT ANALYSIS
This document details customer health metrics and support insights gathered across Q1 2026. We analyzed 150 customer reviews and support interactions to identify key pain points and churn risks.

### SEGMENT A: ENTERPRISE CLIENTS (HIGH ARPU)
Enterprise clients are generally satisfied with product performance, rating it 4.5/5. However, they expressed concerns over API rate limits during peak operational hours.
- **Acme Corp (ID: ENT-902)**: Reported hitting API rate limits twice during peak hours, causing a delay in their ETL pipelines. They requested a higher tier with a 50% increase in API throughput.
- **Globex Corporation (ID: ENT-331)**: Encountered rate limit blocks that caused sync failures in their CRM pipeline. They requested single sign-on (SSO) support for better access controls.

### SEGMENT B: MID-MARKET CLIENTS (CHURN RISK: 12%)
Mid-market clients have a higher churn risk (currently estimated at 12%). The primary complaint is the lack of dedicated account manager support.
- **Initech (ID: MID-104)**: Extremely unsatisfied with support response times for critical bugs. Their average ticket resolution time is currently 18 hours. They are actively comparing alternatives.
- **Hooli (ID: MID-705)**: Mentioned they will downgrade their subscription unless we release the automated scheduling and database synchronization features before March 15, 2026.

## 2. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS & TARGET ACTIONS
1. **API Rate Limits**: Immediately increase rate limits for Enterprise customers (Acme Corp & Globex) by 50% to prevent ETL delays.
2. **Dedicated Support**: Assign a dedicated support specialist to high-risk mid-market clients like Initech.
3. **Automated Scheduling**: Roll out the automated scheduling module before March 15, 2026 to appease Hooli.
